When coding in JavaScript, you can use the keywords "var," "let," and "const" to declare variables. It's important to note that a variable declared with "var" can be use globally, while a variable declared with "let" or "const" has block scope. This means that "let" and "const" variables are only accessible within the block they are declared in.

Here is an explanation of how the scope of var, let, and const work :
1.''var'' declarations can be scoped globally.
2."let" and "const" are block-scoped, which implies that they exist only within a specific block of code. accessible within the block in which they are declared.
